Background

Background and Rationale

Ghana’s digital transformation agenda continues to evolve rapidly, driven by increasing adoption of digital services, expansion of broadband infrastructure, growth in digital entrepreneurship, and rising demand for trusted, inclusive, and scalable technology solutions across all sectors of the economy. The National Information Technology Agency (NITA), as Ghana’s ICT regulator and a key driver of national digital public infrastructure, has over the years provided leadership in coordinating national ICT initiatives and convening stakeholders across the digital ecosystem.

The National ICT Week Celebration has become a flagship annual platform for showcasing innovation, stimulating dialogue, strengthening partnerships, and aligning public and private sector efforts towards Ghana’s digital development goals. Building on lessons from previous editions, the 2026 ICT Week Celebration adopts a refreshed approach that positions NITA primarily as a curator and ecosystem orchestrator, while enabling industry, sector players, innovators, academia, civil society, and development partners to actively plug into and co-create the programme.

The 2026 theme, “INNOVATION AT SCALE: CREATING OPPORTUNITIES ACROSS GHANA’S DIGITAL ECOSYSTEM”, reflects Ghana’s transition from pilots and isolated solutions to platform-based, interoperable, and scalable digital systems that can deliver nationwide impact, attract investment, and create sustainable opportunities for citizens and businesses. This theme was selected to deliberately open the ICT Week platform to all technology-driven sectors and enable each to demonstrate how innovation at scale can accelerate socio-economic development across Ghana.

Curated Approach

Curated Ecosystem-Based Approach

Unlike previous editions, ICT Week Celebration 2026 will adopt a sector-plug-in model. NITA will define the overarching theme, standards, governance framework, and programme structure, while industry sectors and ecosystem players will design and host aligned sessions within the broader celebration.

Role of NITA

NITA will:

  • Curate the overall theme, programme architecture, and quality standards.
  • Provide regulatory, policy, and digital public infrastructure perspectives.
  • Coordinate participating sectors and ensure thematic alignment.
  • Facilitate cross-sector dialogues and high-level plenaries.
  • Ensure coherence, inclusion, and national relevance of all activities.
