Background

Background and Rationale

Ghana's digital transformation agenda continues to accelerate through increased adoption of digital services, expansion of broadband infrastructure, growth in digital entrepreneurship, and rising demand for trusted, inclusive, and scalable technology solutions. National ICT Week is the flagship platform convening stakeholders to translate this momentum into nationwide opportunity.

Theme

Innovation at Scale

The 2026 theme advances the 2025 focus on digital trade foundations by shifting from positioning to execution and scale. It emphasizes interoperable, platform-based solutions that deliver impact across all technology-driven sectors.

Theme for 2026

Creating Opportunities Across Ghana's Digital Ecosystem

From pilots and silos to platforms and national scale.

Objectives

Objectives of ICT Week 2026

Curate the Ecosystem

Position NITA as the national curator, providing governance, standards, and coordination.

Showcase Scalable Solutions

Highlight digital solutions ready for national adoption across multiple sectors.

Enable Interoperability

Promote cross-sector collaboration and interoperable digital platforms.

Create Opportunity

Unlock investment, partnerships, market access, and skills development.

Build Digital Trust

Deepen understanding of national digital public infrastructure and trust frameworks.

Inclusive Participation

Support youth, startups, SMEs, women, and underserved communities.

Curated Approach

Sector Plug-In Model

ICT Week 2026 adopts a curated, ecosystem-based approach. NITA defines the overarching theme, standards, and programme structure while industry sectors co-create aligned sessions within the broader celebration.

NITA's Role

Curate the theme, provide governance, coordinate stakeholders, and ensure inclusion and quality.

Sector Leadership

Industry, academia, innovators, and partners design sessions that scale sector solutions.

Ecosystem Co-Creation

Cross-sector collaboration to build interoperable, national-scale platforms.

Programme

Programme Structure and Activities

Plenary Sessions

Opening and closing ceremonies, high-level policy dialogues, and keynote addresses.

Sector-Led Sessions

Panels, expert talks, technical presentations, and case studies by sector leaders.

Exhibitions and Showcases

Workshops, hackathons, mentorship, and digital career activities.
