Conference Overview Program

Theme in 2026

Innovation at Scale: Creating Opportunities Across Ghana’s Digital Ecosystem

Background and Rationale

Ghana’s digital transformation agenda continues to evolve rapidly, driven by increasing adoption of digital services, expansion of broadband infrastructure, growth in digital entrepreneurship, and rising demand for trusted, inclusive, and scalable technology solutions across all sectors of the economy. The National Information Technology Agency (NITA), as Ghana’s ICT regulator and a key driver of national digital public infrastructure, has over the years provided leadership in coordinating national ICT initiatives and convening stakeholders across the digital ecosystem. The National ICT Week Celebration has become a flagship annual platform for showcasing innovation, stimulating dialogue, strengthening partnerships, and aligning public and private sector efforts towards Ghana’s digital development goals.

Building on lessons from previous editions, the 2026 ICT Week Celebration adopts a refreshed approach that positions NITA primarily as a curator and ecosystem orchestrator, while enabling industry, sector players, innovators, academia, civil society, and development partners to actively plug into and co-create the programme. The 2026 theme, “INNOVATION AT SCALE: CREATING OPPORTUNITIES ACROSS GHANA’S DIGITAL ECOSYSTEM”, reflects Ghana’s transition from pilots and isolated solutions to platform-based, interoperable, and scalable digital systems that can deliver nationwide impact, attract investment, and create sustainable opportunities for citizens and businesses. This theme was selected to deliberately open the ICT Week platform to all technology-driven sectors and enable each to demonstrate how innovation at scale can accelerate socio-economic development across Ghana.

Theme Interpretation

The 2026 theme, “Innovation at Scale: Creating Opportunities Across Ghana’s Digital Ecosystem”, is intentionally designed as a direct progression from the 2025 ICT Week Celebration theme, “Ghana as Africa’s Digital Trade Hub: Innovation, Policy, and Partnerships for the Future.”

The 2025 ICT Week Celebration positioned Ghana within the continental digital economy, focusing on how innovation, enabling policies, and strategic partnerships could establish the country as Africa’s preferred hub for digital trade. That year’s discussions centered on foundational enablers such as digital identity, digital payments, data exchange, digital trust frameworks, cross-border trade systems, and regulatory collaboration—laying the groundwork for Ghana’s role in regional and pan-African digital commerce.

Building on these foundations, the 2026 theme shifts the narrative from positioning to execution and scale. Having articulated the vision of Ghana as a digital trade hub and strengthened the policy, trust, and partnership frameworks required to support that vision, the focus now turns to scaling innovation across the entire digital ecosystem—beyond trade-specific use cases and into all technology-driven sectors of the economy.

“Innovation at Scale” reflects the need to move from pilots, sector silos, and isolated platforms toward interoperable, platform-based, and nationally scalable digital solutions that can serve government, businesses, and citizens alike. “Creating Opportunities” captures the practical outcomes of this scale: expanded markets, job creation, investment flows, startup growth, productivity gains, and improved service delivery across sectors such as health, agriculture, education, finance, human capital development, gaming, and the creative economy.

In this context, ICT Week Celebration 2026 becomes the ecosystem-wide operationalisation of the digital trade foundations discussed in 2025. The trusted digital infrastructure, policy coherence, and partnerships emphasized last year now enable broader sector participation, deeper industry leadership, and cross-sector innovation at national scale.

This thematic evolution ensures continuity and coherence across editions of ICT Week—moving Ghana from a conversation about where it wants to lead in Africa’s digital economy, to a practical demonstration of how innovation, when scaled across the ecosystem, creates concrete opportunities for national and regional development.

Objectives of the ICT Week Celebration 2026

The key objectives of the ICT Week Celebration 2026 are to:

1. Position NITA as the national curator of Ghana’s digital ecosystem, providing governance, standards, and coordination while enabling industry-led innovation.

2. Showcase scalable digital solutions across multiple sectors of the economy.

3. Promote cross-sector collaboration and interoperability among technology platforms.

4. Create opportunities for investment, partnerships, market access, and skills development.

5. Deepen stakeholder understanding of national digital public infrastructure, digital trust, and regulatory frameworks.

6. Encourage inclusive participation of youth, startups, SMEs, women, and underserved communities in Ghana’s digital economy.
